Policy Objectives and Public Health Goals

TAMA enshrines several guiding principles in its Declaration of Policy:

  1. Integrate traditional and alternative healthcare into the national health system.
  2. Encourage scientific research to evaluate the safety, effectiveness, and cost-efficiency of traditional modalities.
  3. Promote ethical standards in the preparation, marketing, and use of herbal products

Creation and Role of PITAHC

A major legacy of the law was establishing the Philippine Institute of Traditional and Alternative Health Care (PITAHC)â€”a government agency under the Department of Health. PITAHC:

  • Leads scientific research and development of traditional medicine.
  • Sets standards and codes of practice for herbal and alternative therapies.
  • Oversees training, accreditation, quality control, and ethical conduct of practitioners and manufacturers.
  • Promotes integration of traditional medicine into national health policies

Regulation and FDA Partnership

Under TAMA, herbal medicines must adhere to rigorous guidelines covering:

  • Clinical evaluation: Validated via scientific research to confirm safety and efficacy.
  • Manufacturing standards: Enforced through Philippine current Good Manufacturing Practices for Natural Products (PCGMPâ€‘NP), benchmarked against WHO standards
  • Product registration and marketing: All herbal medicines must obtain FDA approval and meet labeling and packaging regulations. Once approved, they're listed in the National Traditional Medicine Formulary

Protection of Indigenous Knowledge

TAMA takes a culturally sensitive stance by:

  • Recognizing the rights of indigenous communities over their traditional knowledge, giving them authority over how it's used.
  • Ensuring that any commercial utilization of indigenous herbal knowledge requires proper acknowledgment and equitable benefit-sharing
